# Psychology Reading Map v1

## Objective

Build a layered, evidence-aware psychology base from classical schools to modern validation.

## Track A - Classical Core Texts

- Freud: introductory psychoanalytic corpus (structural model, defense, dream-work baseline).
- Jung: analytical psychology and archetypal/individuation framework.
- Adler: individual psychology and social interest orientation.
- Berne: transactional analysis baseline (`Parent/Adult/Child`).
- Fromm: freedom, conformity, social character analysis.

## Track B - Adjacent Foundational Authors

- Horney: neurotic trends, anxiety and relational coping.
- Klein: object-relations early dynamics.
- Bowlby: attachment theory foundational framework.
- Frankl: logotherapy and meaning under constraint.

## Track C - Modern Validation Layer

- Systematic reviews/meta-analyses on:
  - psychodynamic therapy outcomes,
  - attachment predictive validity,
  - transactional-analysis empirical status,
  - meaning-centered interventions.
  - object-relations measurement vs treatment-effect evidence.

## Track D - Cross-Cultural and Transfer Limits

- Culture-sensitive psychology and interpretation risks.
- Country-specific adaptation constraints.
- Conditions where theory transfer is invalid without local evidence.

## Ingestion Protocol

For each source:
- identify `claim class` (historical / empirical / interpretive);
- capture `operational implication`;
- define `falsification trigger`;
- define `transfer boundary`;
- map to world: `psychology.*`, `culture.*`, or `socio-technical.*`.

## Batch Cadence

- Large mode: 8-15 sources per pass.
- Promote only after quality-gate checks in `playbook-psychology-core-models-v1.md`.

## Initial Source Priority

1. Freud, Jung, Adler, Berne, Fromm (canonical baseline).
2. Bowlby + modern attachment reviews.
3. Frankl/Horney/Klein with caution and modern cross-check.
4. Cross-cultural validity and transfer constraints.

- Track C: in progress (first validation pass completed).
