## Fundamentals

**Thesis:** **Concrete–Representational–Abstract (CRA)** sequence produces **very large** effects on mathematics outcomes vs baseline phases in SCD studies.

**Methods:** Systematic review + meta-analysis of **30 SCD studies** (Tau-BC effect size metric for single-case research).

**Key result:** pooled **Tau-BC = 0.9965** (near-maximal improvement across phases); authors conclude CRA is **evidence-based** for students with/at risk for mathematics learning disabilities.

**CRA sequence:**

1. **Concrete** — manipulatives / physical models
2. **Representational** — drawings, diagrams, number lines
3. **Abstract** — symbolic notation and procedures

**Moderators (from abstract):** grade band, domain (e.g. fractions, operations), fidelity — see full paper for subgroup tables.
