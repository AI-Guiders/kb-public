## Fundamentals

Rosenshine distilled **10 principles** from: (a) cognitive science on working memory; (b) studies of master teachers (high success rate); (c) cognitive supports (models, scaffolds).

| # | Principle (condensed) | Operational signal |
|---|----------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Begin with review | spaced retrieval before new material |
| 2 | New material in small steps | one construct per instructional moment |
| 3 | Ask questions | check understanding, not monologue |
| 4 | Provide models | worked example before problem |
| 5 | Guide student practice | success rate high during acquisition |
| 6 | Check responses | detect misconceptions early |
| 7 | Obtain high success rate | ~80% correct during guided practice (cited range in article) |
| 8 | Provide scaffolds | temporary supports, faded |
| 9 | Independent practice | after sufficient guided success |
| 10 | Weekly/monthly review | cumulative spacing |

## Core unit

- context: designing subject instruction or agent gate curriculum
- signal: lesson too large; no review; no model before problem
- action: map step to Rosenshine #; add held-out probe (see `kb-pedagogy-evidence-global-v1.md`)
- outcome: higher success rate on **new** items, not just immediate recall of taught example
- lesson: explicit instruction ≠ rote; includes questioning and fading

## Operationalization

- first_adoption_task: label each curriculum moment with Rosenshine principle #
- validation_check: held-out probe exists for target construct
- success_criterion: agent/journal shows spacing + independent probe pass
- rollback_or_mitigation: if drill without transfer — add worked example + new context probe
