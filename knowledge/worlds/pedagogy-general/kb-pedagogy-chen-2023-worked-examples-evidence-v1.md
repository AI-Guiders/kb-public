## Fundamentals

**Search:** 8033 abstracts screened → 43 articles, **55 studies**, **181 effect sizes** (experimental/quasi-experimental).

**Primary result:** average **Hedges g = 0.48** [95% CI 0.36–0.60], p = .01 (medium effect, Cohen norms).

**Distribution:** 139 positive, 4 zero, 38 negative effect sizes — heterogeneity present (τ² ≈ 0.59).

**Moderators (significant / notable):**

| Moderator | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| **Example type** | Correct examples alone > incorrect alone or correct+incorrect mix |
| **Self-explanation prompts** | **Negative** vs no prompts — pairing may hurt vs examples alone |
| **Timing** | Effective for **practice** and **initial acquisition** (both) |
| Grade band | See full paper subgroups |

**CLT link:** worked examples reduce extraneous cognitive load during schema acquisition (Sweller strand); **expertise reversal** still applies at high proficiency.

## Extracted knowledge (K)

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K1 | Worked example before problem-solving is cross-math evidence-based default for novices |
| K2 | Do not bundle self-explanation prompts by default — test locally |
| K3 | Incorrect examples require careful design; correct-only safer for average gain |
| K4 | Applies to von Neumann math, physics, chemistry problem sets — pair with CRA/PRIMM where domain-specific |
| K5 | Fade examples systematically (guidance fading); experts need problems, not more examples |
