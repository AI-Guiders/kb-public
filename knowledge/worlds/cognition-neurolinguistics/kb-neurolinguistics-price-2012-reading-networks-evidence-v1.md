## Fundamentals

**Thesis:** language and reading rely on **distributed, overlapping networks** — not a single Broca/Wernicke pair. Reading dissociates **sublexical**, **lexical**, and **semantic** neural pathways (Marshall & Newcombe 1973 legacy confirmed in imaging).

**Price reading pathway table (conceptual):**

| Pathway | Function | Neural correlate (consistent across studies) |
|---------|----------|---------------------------------------------|
| **Sublexical reading** | GPC / pseudoword route | Left occipito-temporal + posterior temporal + IFG articulatory recoding |
| **Lexical reading** | Known word form → sound | Medial extrastriate / VWFA region + familiar word phonology |
| **Semantic reading** | Meaning access (incl. irregular words) | Ventral temporal + frontal semantic areas |

**Broader principles:**

- Phonological processing of heard speech integrates **auditory + articulatory** systems
- Orthographic processing integrates **visual + articulation + semantics**
- Specialization often emerges from **functional integration** of areas each serving multiple functions — not one-area-one-function only

**Pre-imaging legacy validated:** selective dyslexia patterns (surface vs phonological) predicted **multiple routes** before fMRI.
