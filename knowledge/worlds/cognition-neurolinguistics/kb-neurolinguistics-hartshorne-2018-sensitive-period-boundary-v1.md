## Fundamentals

**Hartshorne et al. (2018) — key numbers (n ≈ 669,498):**

| Claim | Estimate |
|-------|----------|
| Grammar-learning **ability** preserved through childhood | Until ~**17.4 years** then declines |
| **Native-like grammar** (ultimate attainment plateau) | Most likely if L2 start **before 10–12** |
| Asymptotic mastery time | ~**30 years** in immersion-like settings (both native and non-native learners) |
| Easy vs hard syntax | Same age trajectory |

**Method note:** disentangles current age, age at first exposure (AOA), and years of experience — large web sample, English grammar quiz.

## 2022 debate (van der Slik vs Hartshorne) — **do not over-read either side**

| Side | Claim |
|------|--------|
| **van der Slik et al. (2022)** | Sharp drop at **17.4** may be artefact of mixing **non-immersion** (school-only) learners; **immersion-only** subsample → **gradual** decline; learner-type heterogeneity |
| **Hartshorne et al. (2022 rebuttal)** | Reanalysis still supports sharp decline for **each** learner stratum when HTP model refitted; SSBH model comparison does not invalidate original |

**KB stance (2026):** statistical shape of grammar vs age remains **contested** at population level. **Unchanged for von Neumann:** school program oracle = Genesee + Thomas & Collier; Hartshorne/van der Slik inform **aspiration and realism**, not cancel gates. **Deny:** «science settled at 17.4» **and** «no age effects at all».

## School evidence counterweight (Genesee — **pedagogy oracle**)

| Construct | Hartshorne (population grammar) | Genesee (school programs) |
|-----------|--------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Question** | When does **biology-limited grammar ability** fall off? | Do **immersion/dual-language schools** work? |
| **Late start** | Native-like grammar unlikely after ~10–12 | **Late immersion** can reach strong L2; early start ≠ only path for **functional proficiency** |
| **Adolescence** | Ability decline begins ~17 | School L2 still valuable; Thomas & Collier 6-year dual-language models |
| **Operational use** | Inform **start-age aspiration** for native-like grammar | Inform **program structure**, hours, L1 anchor |

**Synthesis for von Neumann:**

- **Aspire** to meaningful EN exposure **before 10–12** if **native-like grammar** is north-star — supports early second-native track, not FL delay.
- **Do not deny** quality bilingual/immersion for learners who start later — Genesee late immersion data + proficiency gains.
- **Do not cite** Hartshorne to **cancel** secondary EN or to claim «brain closed at 7» — neuromyth deny.
