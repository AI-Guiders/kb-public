## Fundamentals

**Thesis:** growing up bilingual reshapes **language networks + executive regions** from infancy; behavioral parity with monolinguals often hides **distinct neural recruitment**.

**Scope:** **94** peer-reviewed neuro studies (PRISMA); simultaneous, sequential, L2 learners.

| Stage / group | Neural pattern |
|---------------|----------------|
| **Infancy** | Delayed perceptual narrowing (maintain L1+L2 phonetic contrasts longer) |
| **Early immersive bilinguals** | Comparable behavior; **extra frontal/executive** activation vs monolinguals |
| **L2 learners (school)** | ERP/fMRI **mature** with input + proficiency; more language-region activation over time |
| **Structure** | White/grey matter differences; ongoing refinement (not static) |

**Debates flagged (2026 review):**

- **Bilingual cognitive advantage:** large adult/child datasets → **null** in many analyses (Nichols 2020; Dick 2019); publication bias concerns — **do not** sell second-native on EF hype alone
- **Consensus retained:** bilingualism **does** alter language-processing networks; control regions often co-activated

**Models referenced:** Green & Abutalebi Adaptive Control; Pliatsikas Dynamic Restructuring — adult frameworks applied cautiously to development.
