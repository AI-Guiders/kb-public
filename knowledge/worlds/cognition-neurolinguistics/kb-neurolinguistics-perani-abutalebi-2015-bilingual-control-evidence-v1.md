## Fundamentals

**Thesis:** bilinguals do **not** store languages in isolated boxes — **both languages remain active**; **language control network** (domain-general executive + subcortical) selects, monitors, and inhibits non-target language during production.

**Control network (Green & Abutalebi adaptive control model):**

| Region | Role in bilingual control |
|--------|---------------------------|
| **dACC / pre-SMA** | Supervisory monitoring; local control (recent L2 trial → switch to L1) |
| **Left IFG (LIFG)** | Response **selection** in target language |
| **Right IFG** | Response **inhibition** (domain-general overlap) |
| **Left caudate / putamen** | Language switching, engagement/disengagement |
| **Inferior parietal** | Selection under interference |
| **Temporal poles** | Lexical/semantic — L2 proficiency correlates with grey matter (aging studies) |

**Proficiency effects:**

- **Prefrontal effect:** higher L2 proficiency → **decreased** prefrontal activation (more automatic L2)
- **Age of acquisition (AOA):** early high-proficient bilinguals show **different** neural organization for L1/L2 than late learners — both languages mapped, patterns differ by AOA and daily usage (Perani 2003)

**Implication for second-native pedagogy:** long-term dual-language exposure trains **control + representation** — aligns with Genesee/Thomas & Collier **school** evidence, explains why minimal FL hours fail to build native-like control.
