## Fundamentals

**Thesis:** literacy **recycles** left ventral visual cortex into **Visual Word Form Area (VWFA)** — a script-specific «letterbox» — and builds a **bidirectional circuit** linking orthography to spoken-language phonology.

**Reading flow (expert, ~170 ms to VWFA):**

```
Visual cortex → VWFA (orthographic code) → phonological areas + semantic areas
```

**Key neural findings:**

| Finding | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| VWFA at left occipito-temporal sulcus | Cross-script bottleneck for skilled reading |
| VWFA lesions → pure alexia | Orthographic identification necessary |
| Literacy ↑ planum temporale response to speech | Grapheme–phoneme conversion route develops with reading |
| Arcuate fasciculus (VWFA–temporal) correlates with literacy | White-matter route for GPC |
| **Visual exposure alone insufficient** | VWFA specialization requires **systematic print–speech mapping** |
| Dyslexia: weak VWFA specialization | Often co-occurs with phonological deficit (majority) |

**Dehaene on education (2024 Reading League):** neuroscience **converges** with NRP/Ehri/Castles — explicit grapheme–phoneme teaching most efficient; neuro does not replace behavioral evidence.
