## Fundamentals

**Thesis:** **Interactive engagement (IE)** courses show substantially higher **normalized gain** `<g>` on concept inventories than **traditional (T)** lecture + standard recitation.

**Sample:** 62 introductory mechanics courses, ~6542 students; `<g>` = (post% − pre%) / (100% − pre%).

**Key aggregates (Hake 1998, widely cited):**

| Method | Mean `<g>` | SD (approx.) | n courses |
|--------|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| Interactive engagement | **0.48** | ~0.14 | 48 |
| Traditional | **0.23** | ~0.04 | 14 |

**Statistics:** difference highly significant (t ≈ 9.9, p ≪ 0.001); IE `<g>` SD larger → method implementation quality varies.

**IE examples:** Peer Instruction, Tutorials in Intro Physics, Workshop Physics, Modeling Instruction, others with in-class engagement + concept focus.

## Operationalization (physics v1)
