## Fundamentals

**Thesis:** **Modeling Instruction** organizes mechanics as **model development → deployment** cycles grounded in student-designed experiments and explicit representational tools (diagrams, graphs, equations).

**Two-stage macro-cycle:**

1. **Model development** — lab-derived models of target phenomenon (e.g. constant velocity, uniform acceleration)
2. **Model deployment** — apply models to new contexts, problems, and testing

**Micro modeling cycle (within development):** model elicitation → model development (labs) → model deployment → model revision as needed.

**Fig. 1 evidence (Mechanics Diagnostic, mean class %):**

| Course | Pretest → Posttest | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| HS Cooperative inquiry (1982–83) | 31 → 57 | Activity-heavy, pre-modeling |
| HS **Modeling** (1986–87) | 38 → **72** | Inquiry + modeling structure + misconception agenda |
| HS Traditional control | 37 → 45 | Lecture 80%, lab 20% |

**Key contrast:** modeling beat **cooperative inquiry alone** — coherence from modeling agenda and taxonomy of misconceptions, not merely hands-on time (both ~70% lab).
