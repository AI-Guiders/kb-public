## 1. Устойчивое извлечённое знание

### A. Theory & definitions

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K1** | **Negative input** = information that an utterance is ungrammatical; confined to «unlearning» / shedding errors, not initial acquisition (Saxton 1997) | vs positive input (grammatical models) |
| **K2** | **Negative evidence (NE)** = adult response **directly contingent** on child grammatical error with **immediate contrast** between error and correct alternative (cf. Direct Contrast) | Coding definition (7) in paper |
| **K3** | Contrast theory: error-contingent models supply **both** (a) grammatical adult form and (b) information that **child form is ungrammatical** via juxtaposition in shared discourse context | Examples: *womans*→*women*, *bored*→*boring* |
| **K4** | Child must perceive **functional equivalence** between error and correction; spurious contrasts (different referents/functions) should not trigger retreat | Discusses constructed counterexamples (5)(6) |
| **K5** | ~20+ studies report negative evidence in input; frequencies vary widely (e.g. Farrar 1992: 3% past vs 44% articles; Chouinard & Clark 2003 up to 65%) | Availability established; not uniform |

### B. Design & sample

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K6** | **N=12** mother–child pairs recruited (mean age **2;0**, range 1;9–2;5; MLU mean 2.31); **10** retained for analysis (exclude MLU<2.0 at T1 and/or MLU gain <1.0 over 12 weeks) | Homogeneity filter |
| **K7** | **40 h** transcribed data: 2 h × 2 visits × 2 time points; **T1–T2 lag ~12 weeks** (±9 days); home naturalistic play | West London, English-only, middle class |
| **K8** | **13** target structures coded; **11** analyzed (insufficient data: irregular plural, regular past) | Mirrors Farrar 1992 / Saxton 2000 categories |
| **K9** | Three input types at T1: **negative evidence**, **contingent models** (adult models target after **grammatical** child use), **non-contingent models** (all other adult models of target, excluding NE) | Tests contrast theory vs nativist «subset of positive input» |
| **K10** | Coding reliability (20% recode): NE **93.9%**, non-contingent **95.7%**, contingent **96.5%** agreement | Proctor-Williams et al. 2001 method |
| **K11** | Analysis: correlate **NI at T1** with **child grammaticality at T2**; partial out CG1; hierarchical regression NE1+CG1→CG2; Richards residual-gain approach | Addresses child→mother feedback loop |

### C. Developmental change (T1→T2)

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K12** | MLU: **2.45** (SD 0.46) → **3.25** (SD 0.55), *t*(10)=4.83, *p*<.0005 | Global grammar gain over 12 weeks |
| **K13** | **Percentage correct in obligatory contexts** increased for **all 11 structures except irregular past** (-4.3 pp) | Enables structure-specific NI tests |

### D. Input frequencies

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K14** | NE frequencies **decline T1→T2** as child errors decrease; **positive models** do not show parallel decline | Expected with improving child grammar |
| **K15** | Non-contingent models **far exceed** NE (e.g. subjects modeled ~**24×** more often as non-contingent than as NE at T1) | Even «low» NE counts can imply ~34/week on 40h talk-week estimate |
| **K16** | ~**57–59%** of morphological errors meet NE at T1; syntax (subject/object) ~**75%** at T1, ~**68%** at T2 | Subject omissions especially often corrected (adults rarely omit subjects) |

### E. Main results (12-week effects)

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K17** | **Three structures**: significant **NI1 × CG2** correlations — **possessive** *r*=**.84**, **3rd person singular** *r*=**.77**, **copula** *r*=**.85** (*p*<.01) | Raw correlations |
| **K18** | Partial correlations (CG1 partialed) preserved: possessive **.77**, 3PS **.74**, copula **.79** (*p*<.01); power ~**.71–.74** | Small N but robust effect sizes |
| **K19** | Regression: NE1 alone predicts CG2 — 3PS **53.3%** variance (β=.70); copula **64.8%** (β=.78); possessive **41.8%** (β=.70); CG1 dropped as non-significant | Caution: N=10 regression |
| **K20** | **No** significant NI1×CG2 for other 8 structures; **non-contingent** and **contingent** positive models: correlations **-.15 to .37** (uniformly low) | Supports special status of error-contingent contrast vs positive-only |
| **K21** | Copula finding **replicates** Proctor-Williams et al. (2001) naturalistic control group (*r*≈.63–.87 depending on measure); determiner/article **null** here (*r*=.14) matches their ~.09 | Cross-study triangulation |

### F. Interpretation & limits (authors)

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K22** | Selective effects (3/13) may reflect methodology (sampling net), developmental timing per structure, or true insensitivity — perceptual salience (-s) **insufficient** alone (regular plural null) | Authors discuss alternatives |
| **K23** | Negative input may be **facilitative but not necessary** for acquisition; still relevant for **language delay intervention** | Concluding remarks |
| **K24** | Critique of Chouinard & Clark (2003): **Direct Contrast** (form juxtaposition in discourse) ≠ Clark **Principle of Contrast** (lexical form–meaning pairs in language) | Theoretical section |
| **K25** | «No negative evidence» assumption weakens poverty-of-stimulus **evaluation metric** for competing UG accounts (Saxton et al. 1998 argument) | Nativism debate framing |

## 2. Ограничения

- **Small N** (10); correlational design — causation inferred via lag + partialing, not experiment.
- Age **~2;0**, English middle-class dyads — not general L1 at all ages/cultures.
- Structure-specific positives may be **false negatives** due to power / sampling.
- Distinct from Saxton (1997) **novel-verb lab** paradigm at 5;0 — complementary evidence line.

## 3. Связь с CASA (hypothesis only)

| Finding | Design question (not spec) |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Only error-contingent NE predicts 12-week gain for 3/11 structures | Should teacher/sleep contrast be **structure-tagged** not global? |
| Positive contingent + non-contingent models null at lag | Bench: «more input» ≠ «contrastive correction» |
| NE frequency drops as errors drop | Oracle timing: correction **when error occurs**, not on schedule |
| Functional-equivalence filter for contrast | Avoid spurious reformulations across different intents |
