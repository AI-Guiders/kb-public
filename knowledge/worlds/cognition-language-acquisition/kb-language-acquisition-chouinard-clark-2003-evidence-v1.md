## 1. Устойчивое извлечённое знание

### A. Theory

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K1** | Critique **reply-type only** (Demetras et al.): expansion after grammatical child utterance looks like expansion after error — cannot locate error (ex. *I went to school* vs *I go to school*) | Reply-type insufficient for error locus |
| **K2** | **Reformulation** = adult repeats child portion **in corrected form** at **locus of error**; via **side sequence** (Jefferson 1972) or **embedded correction** (Jefferson 1982) | Primary function: check child **intention**, not deliberate teaching |
| **K3** | Same adult sentence can be **positive** (model) or **negative evidence** (reformulation after error) depending on **pragmatics** of exchange | Duality by conversational role |
| **K4** | Child has two forms for **same intended meaning** → **Principle of Contrast** (Clark 1987/1990/1993) + **Conventionality** → defer to adult conventional form | Meaning-same, form-different → discard non-conventional |
| **K5** | Adults reformulate to **understand** child; if misunderstood, child **rejects** reformulation (ex. Abe *plant didn't cried* → father wrong → *no*) | Clarification-first, not correction-first |
| **K6** | Contrast at error locus is informative — **not** statistical reply-type differential alone | Aligns partially with Saxton on juxtaposition; differs on **meaning** vs **form** emphasis |

### B. Methods

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K7** | **5 corpora** CHILDES: Abe, Sarah, Naomi (EN); Philippe, Grégoire (FR); spontaneous speech **2;0–4;0** (Grégoire to 2;5) | Longitudinal, parent speech only |
| **K8** | Errors coded: phonological, morphological, lexical, syntactic; next adult turn = **reformulation** if corrected repeat of erroneous portion | vs **replay** on 200 random conventional utterances per age slice |
| **K9** | Reliability: conventionality κ≈.66; reformulation κ≈.81; error type κ≈.85; child response κ≈.74–.85 | Two coders, discuss discrepancies |
| **K10** | Coded lines: Abe 6276, Sarah 5029, Naomi 2242, Philippe 2421, Grégoire 5511; erroneous utterances ~1095–2911 per child | Large within-child N |

### C. Results — availability

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K11** | **Reformulations follow erroneous utterances much more often than replays follow conventional ones** (all 5 children, p<.001 log-linear) | Core negative-evidence availability claim |
| **K12** | Up to **~two-thirds** of erroneous utterances reformulated (varies by child/age); lowest ~**48%** (Naomi 2;0–2;5) | Still «more than high enough» per authors (Estes/Levine <25% learning threshold) |
| **K13** | Reformulations for **all error types** (phonology, morphology, lexicon, syntax) in EN+FR; rates broadly comparable across types | Not driven by one category |
| **K14** | **Side sequences** majority: Abe 57%, Sarah/Naomi/Philippe ~70–73%, Grégoire 62% | Check intention before continuing |
| **K15** | Reformulation rate **decreases with age** as errors decrease (Abe, Naomi, Sarah, Philippe significant) | Not constant % of remaining errors |

### D. Results — child use

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K16** | Child next-turn responses: **overt uptake** (repeat correction) **10–50%**; **rejection** up to ~14%; **acknowledgement** (uh-huh/yeah) 2–51%; **repeat new info** 1–17%; **bare continuation** 25–80% | Multiple attention channels |
| **K17** | Uptake often **incorporates change in new utterance**, not verbatim repeat (ex. Abe adds *on* after father *put butter on it*) | Processing, not mimicry only |
| **K18** | Acknowledgement + continuation treated as evidence child monitors whether adult captured **intended meaning** | Tacit acceptance ≠ ignoring |

### E. Discussion / cross-literature

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K19** | Cites Saxton 1997/1998: **<20 exposures** with negative evidence enough for novel verbs — present corpus rates «more than high enough» | Bridges to Saxton program |
| **K20** | **Saxton (2005 Negative643)** critique in related work: Clark **Principle of Contrast** (lexical form–meaning in language) ≠ Saxton **Direct Contrast** (discourse form juxtaposition) | Theoretical fork — see Saxton 2005 card |
| **K21** | Cross-cultural: Kaluli/Samoan use explicit «say like that»; continuum from reformulation to explicit directive | Negative evidence not only middle-class side sequences |
| **K22** | Parents **unlikely conscious correctors** — reformulation for conversational goals; corrective info **nevertheless available** | Intent vs child-learnable signal |

## 2. Ограничения

- Naturalistic CHILDES — mostly **middle-class** EN/FR; observer present in some corpora.
- **Correlational** — reformulation ↔ eventual acquisition not tracked longitudinally per structure here.
- Phonology under-coded (non-phonetic transcripts).
- Debate with Saxton unresolved in this paper — both cite overlapping data differently.

## 3. Связь с CASA (hypothesis only)

| Finding | Design question |
|---------|-----------------|
| Reformulation >> replay after errors | Teacher should fire on **critic-flagged error**, not on every child surface |
| Side sequence / clarification before model | `lexical_ask` before committed `ortho_surface` — sequence OK |
| Same meaning, contrasting form | Scene commits **intent**; contrast targets **form** at locus |
| 10–50% overt uptake | Low immediate repair **normal**; don't bench solely on instant repeat |
| Child rejects wrong adult guess | Teacher wrong disambiguation must be **rejectable**, not forced sleep merge |
| Not conscious correction | Engineering `correction_kind` ≠ «parent intent detector» |
