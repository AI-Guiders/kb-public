## 1. Извлечённое знание

| # | Тезис | Источник |
|---|--------|----------|
| **K10** | **Contrast theory:** negative evidence = discourse **juxtaposition** child error + adult correct form → child may perceive **conflict/contrast** → basis to reject error | Abstract; Saxton framing vs Brown & Hanlon / Gold |
| **K11** | **Direct Contrast Hypothesis (1997 p. 155):** when child produces erroneous form and adult **immediately** supplies correct alternative, child may treat adult form as **in CONTRAST** with child form → perceive adult form as correct alternative | Quoted in Columbia thesis citing Saxton 1997 |
| **K12** | **Design:** within-subjects; **N=36**, mean age **5;0**; **six novel irregular past tense** forms taught (present tense via video description first) | Abstract; UvA slides |
| **K13** | **Negative evidence condition:** elicit past → child regularizes → adult supplies correct irregular (contrastive) | UvA slides (e.g. *pelled* → *pold*) |
| **K14** | **Positive input condition:** adult provides correct irregular **without** prior child error juxtaposition | UvA slides |
| **K15** | **Results (headline):** children reproduce correct irregular model **more often** and **persist with fewer errors** after **negative evidence** than after positive input alone | Abstract; UvA slides |
| **K16** | 1997 reports **immediate** effects only; **12-week** naturalistic extension → [`kb-language-acquisition-saxton-2005-negative-evidence-v1.md`](kb-language-acquisition-saxton-2005-negative-evidence-v1.md) | Separate paper |
| **K17** | **Compound (2005 Negative643 full):** positive input alone insufficient for «unlearning»; NE supplies ungrammaticality info via functional equivalence + juxtaposition | Saxton 2005 §2–3 (re-states 1997 theory with examples *womans*→*women*) |
