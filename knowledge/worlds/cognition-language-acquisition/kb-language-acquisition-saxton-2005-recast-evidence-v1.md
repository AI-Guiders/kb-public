## 1. Устойчивое извлечённое знание

| # | Тезис | Суть |
|---|--------|------|
| **K23** | **Recast** (Bohannon et al. 1996): adult reflects child utterance with overlap in meaning but may expand/delete/permute form | Conversational maintenance, not inherently grammatical correction |
| **K24** | **Differential responding** (recasts follow errors **more often**, not exclusively) **insufficient** as corrective mechanism | Child would vacillate; category «recast» too nebulous for young learner |
| **K25** | **Contrast theory subset:** only **error-contingent** adult models with **direct juxtaposition** of child error + adult correct form (Direct Contrast) | Examples: *bited*→*bit*, *interesting*→*interested*; recast label irrelevant |
| **K26** | Direct Contrast yields: (a) what is grammatical, (b) that child form is ungrammatical | Applies to commission **and** omission errors (e.g. missing article) |
| **K27** | **Four empirical themes** (citing Farrar 1992, Saxton 1997/2000/1998, Strapp, Chouinard & Clark 2003): error-contingent models > non-contingent; immediate + longer-term effects; naturalistic multiple categories; novel-verb experiment isolates negative evidence | Review synthesis |
| **K28** | **Broad recast** interventions for LD **equivocal**; success may track **Direct Contrast** subset, not all recasts | Nelson simple vs complex recast definitions ≠ Direct Contrast |
| **K29** | Demetras et al. (1986) cited among TL studies of differential responding to well/ill-formed utterances | Links to [`kb-language-acquisition-demetras-1986-evidence-v1.md`](kb-language-acquisition-demetras-1986-evidence-v1.md) |
