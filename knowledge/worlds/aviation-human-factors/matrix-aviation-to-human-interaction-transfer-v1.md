# Aviation -> Human Interaction Transfer Matrix v1

## Purpose

Transfer useful safety and coordination patterns from aviation to everyday human interaction without importing aviation-only assumptions.

## Transfer Contract

- Transfer **principles**, not procedures tied to cockpit regulation.
- Keep epistemic labels explicit: `fact`, `inference`, `hypothesis`.
- Route through [`../../matrix-culture-routing-v1.md`](../../matrix-culture-routing-v1.md) before local application.
- Apply [`../../matrix-do-not-transfer-v1.md`](../../matrix-do-not-transfer-v1.md) for hard boundaries.

## Matrix

| Aviation pattern | Transferable core | Everyday interaction example | Boundary check |
|---|---|---|---|
| CRM cross-check | distributed verification beats solo certainty | ask one explicit confirmation before high-impact decisions | avoid turning every conversation into bureaucracy |
| TEM (threat/error management) | identify likely error paths before action | pre-brief risks before a difficult dialogue | do not present risk list as accusation |
| Sterile-cockpit discipline | protect focus during critical phase | suppress side topics in conflict resolution windows | do not overextend to normal social talk |
| Readback/hearback | close loop on instruction meaning | repeat key agreement in own words | avoid mechanical repetition in emotionally sensitive moments |
| Just Culture | distinguish error, drift, and negligence | discuss outcome + context before blame | no "no-blame" shield for repeated harmful behavior |
| Go-around mindset | abort unsafe path early | pause escalation when signals degrade | pause must include explicit resume/replan point |
| Checklist discipline | external memory reduces omission risk | use lightweight checklist for recurring joint tasks | checklist complements, not replaces understanding |
| Fatigue awareness | degraded cognition needs compensation | defer hard decisions under exhaustion | do not weaponize "you are tired" rhetoric |

## Non-Transfer Zones

- Legal/regulatory aviation constraints do not automatically map to civil conversation contexts.
- Authority gradients in cockpit crews are not equivalent to family/team power structures.
- Incident-severity terminology should be normalized for non-aviation settings.

## Validation Snapshot v1

- Pattern transfer is highest-value in high-stakes, time-constrained, ambiguity-heavy interactions.
- Misuse risk rises when aviation language is copied literally instead of translated to intent-level rules.
