## Fundamentals

**Thesis:** cognitively beneficial bilingualism requires **adequate L1 foundation**; L2 academic competence is **partially a function of L1 competence** at onset of intensive L2 (developmental interdependence).

**Two hypotheses:**

| Hypothesis | Claim |
|------------|-------|
| **Developmental interdependence** | L2 competence ↑ with L1 CALP at start of L2 intensive exposure |
| **Threshold** | Minimum L1/L2 levels needed to avoid cognitive disadvantage and unlock bilingual benefits |

**BICS vs CALP:** conversational fluency (BICS) develops faster; **decontextualized academic language (CALP)** underlies literacy and cross-linguistic transfer (common underlying proficiency / «dual iceberg»).

**Policy evidence cited:** for minority children, **L1-medium instruction** can promote **L2** outcomes ≥ L2-only short programs — supports second-native **L1 anchor**, not subtractive bilingualism.
