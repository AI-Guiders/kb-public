## Fundamentals

**Thesis:** **Geo-literacy** = capacity to make **far-reaching decisions** using geographic knowledge — three components (**three I's**):

1. **Interactions** — how human and physical systems interact **in places** (e.g. agriculture + climate + economy)
2. **Interconnections** — how places connect across scales (trade, migration, teleconnections)
3. **Implications** — reasoning about **consequences** of choices for self and others (sustainability, equity)

**Spatial thinking (Edelson + NRC 2006):** maps, GIS, and diagrams are **reasoning tools** — spatial literacy includes interpreting scale, projection, layering, not memorizing capitals alone.
