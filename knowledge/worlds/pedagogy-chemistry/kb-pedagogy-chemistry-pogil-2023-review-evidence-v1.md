## Fundamentals

**Thesis:** **Inquiry-based chemistry** in reviewed literature clusters around **POGIL** (Process Oriented Guided Inquiry Learning) and related guided-inquiry cycles — not unstructured discovery.

**POGIL structure (Jegstad synthesis):**

1. **Exploration** — guided activity sheet, teams
2. **Concept invention / formation** — debrief, term introduction
3. **Application** — transfer problems

**Team roles:** manager, spokesperson, recorder, strategy analyst — interdependence by design (Hanson 2006).

**Representative findings from included studies:**

| Study strand | Outcome |
|--------------|---------|
| Particulate nature (Barthlow & Watson 2014) | Fewer alternative conceptions vs lecture |
| Electrochemistry (Sen et al. 2016) | Better understanding, fewer misconceptions |
| Organic chemistry confidence (De Gale & Boisselle 2015) | Mixed performance, **↑ academic confidence** |
| Middle school matter (Özkanbaş & Kırık 2020) | Positive conceptual gains |

**Moog et al. (2017) meta (21 studies, n = 7876):** achievement **g+ = 0.29** [0.15–0.43]; **odds of passing ×2.02**; fail risk ↓38%.
