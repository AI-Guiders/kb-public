## Fundamentals

**Orthographic mapping (OM):** formation of letter-sound connections bonding **spelling ↔ pronunciation ↔ meaning** in memory → **sight word reading**, spelling memory, vocabulary from print.

**Endpoint for von Neumann RU tract:** fluent whole-word read on field = **outcome of OM**, not bypass of print analysis forever.

**Share self-teaching:** successful decode/analytic read + rereading → words stored; third graders remember letters after **one exposure** (Share 2004b).

---

## Phases (Ehri 2005a; Table 1 in paper)

| Phase | Connections | Read unfamiliar | Spell |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|-------|
| Pre-alphabetic | Visual/context | Predict from context | Nonphonetic |
| Partial alphabetic | Partial GPC | Initial letters + context | Partial invented |
| Full alphabetic | Complete GPC | Decode; limited analogy | Phonetic invented |
| Consolidated alphabetic | Grapho-syllabic/morphemic chunks | Decode/analogy proficient | Proficient |

Phases = **overlapping waves** (Siegler), not rigid stages.

## Extracted knowledge (K)

### Mechanism

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K1 | All practiced words become **sight words** — not limited to high-frequency/irregular lists |
| K2 | OM requires **phonemic segmentation/blending** + **GPC knowledge** (+ grapho-syllabic at advanced) |
| K3 | Connections **glue** spellings to pronunciations; meanings bonded in parallel |
| K4 | Decoding, analogy, prediction are **tools** that feed OM; fluent read **from memory** is target |
| K5 | **Lexical quality** (Perfetti): complete grapho-phonemic bonds → accurate ID + frees resources for comprehension |
| K6 | Spelling in memory **shapes** phonological processing (pitch vs rich segmentation; rhyme RT effects) |

### Instruction — beginners

| # | Тезис | Evidence cited |
|---|--------|----------------|
| K7 | **Articulatory PA** (mouth pictures) > letters-only for sight-word learning | Boyer & Ehri 2011: ~4 vs 8 trials; Castiglioni-Spalten & Ehri 2003 |
| K8 | **Embedded picture mnemonics** (letter shape = object) > disassociated mnemonics | Ehri et al. 1984; Shmidman & Ehri 2010 Hebrew |
| K9 | Letter **names** help partial-phase word learning when GPC known | Ehri & Wilce 1985; Cardoso-Martins et al. 2011 |
| K10 | Teach GPC + segmentation + **decode novel words aloud** in independent reading | Share 2004b; Rosenthal & Ehri 2011 |

### Vocabulary via print

| # | Тезис | Evidence |
|---|--------|----------|
| K11 | Showing **spellings** while teaching spoken vocab improves pronunciation recall | Rosenthal & Ehri 2008; 2nd & 5th grade |
| K12 | Orthographic knowledge explains vocab advantage of good readers more than phonological WM alone | Fig. 3 hi vs lo readers |
| K13 | **Read aloud** underlined novel words during silent reading improves vocab + spelling memory | Rosenthal & Ehri 2011; larger ES for poorer decoders (d≈1.58 vs 0.71 recall) |
| K14 | Imagining spellings helps memory for pronunciations | Ehri & Wilce 1979 Exp. 4 |

### Program design

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K15 | Spontaneous OM when prerequisites met — experimenter need not draw attention to letters if child decodes |
| K16 | Practice patterns: segment+match GPC; word chains (sock→sack→…); multisyllable grapho-syllabic segments |
| K17 | Reciprocal: GPC knowledge → read words → accumulate lexical knowledge → implicit orthotactic/statistical learning |
