## Fundamentals

**Simple View of Reading (SVR):**

```text
RC = D × LC
```

- **RC** — reading comprehension  
- **D** — decoding / efficient word recognition (extends beyond phonics drill to fast accurate word ID in and out of text)  
- **LC** — linguistic comprehension (listening comprehension with parallel materials)

**Logical claim:** both components **necessary**; neither alone sufficient. Multiplicative form: weakness in either caps RC; RC not average of D and LC.

**Gough & Tunmer (1986):** introduced framework; Hoover & Gough (1990) **longitudinal validation**.

---

## Validation (Hoover & Gough 1990)

**Sample:** English–Spanish bilingual children, grades 1–4, multi-site longitudinal (1978–1985); varied reading instruction by site (phonics-emphasis vs whole-word/delayed phonics vs individualized).

**Measures:**

- **D:** nonsense word lists (hin → conspartable difficulty tiers)  
- **LC:** listen to story, retell, oral questions  
- **RC:** parallel materials **read** by child, oral questions  

**Three predictions — all supported:**

| # | Prediction | Result |
|---|------------|--------|
| P1 | D + LC explain RC; **product term** improves fit over linear sum alone | Hierarchical regression: product largest unique contribution |
| P2 | Correlation D↔LC → **negative** when restricting to less skilled readers | Compensatory profiles |
| P3 | LC→RC slope **increases** with D; intercepts ≈ 0 | At D≈0, RC≈0 regardless of LC |

Reading Rockets reports grade 1–4 **RC vs predicted RC correlations ≥ 0.8** when using D×LC.

## Extracted knowledge (K)

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K1 | D and LC are **distinct** — tag probes separately on field |
| K2 | High D, low LC → **“specific comprehension deficit”** / poor language comp profile |
| K3 | High LC, low D → RC capped — classic **dyslexic** pattern on RC |
| K4 | Low both → **garden variety** poor reader; RC lower than either component (multiplier) |
| K5 | Comprehension-only intervention fails when D weak (Reading Rockets intervention vignette) |
| K6 | SVR is **framework not process model** — does not specify how D/LC develop (Castles Box 5) |
| K7 | Construct measurement inconsistent across studies — need precise D (word recognition fluency) vs LC (vocab, inference, memory) |
| K8 | Once D adequate, RC constrained primarily by LC (LARRC 2015; Castles Box 5) |
| K9 | Implications for **reading disability** definition and literacy policy discussed in paper conclusion |

## Operationalization (RU written v1 / von Neumann)

| Profile | Field signal | Tract emphasis |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| Low D | OOV fail, slow word ID | GPC bootstrap → OM; Castles §1 |
| Low C | Words OK, passage fail | Vocab, inference, dialogue (Castles §4) |
| Low both | Both probes fail | Split moments; don’t merge gate |
| High D, low C | Word caller risk | **Do not** reduce D teaching; build LC |

- Gate **must** include held-out **word** and **sentence/passage** where stage spec requires both
- **Do not** use SVR to skip decoding for novices
- Manifest labels (литература vs язык) ≠ D/C construct tags
