## Fundamentals (three-stage model)

| Stage | Focus | End state |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| **§1 Cracking the code** | GPC, PA, systematic phonics | Alphabetic insight + decode unfamiliar words |
| **§2 Skilled word recognition** | Orthographic learning, self-teaching, lexical quality | Fluent word ID; print→meaning without full decode |
| **§3 Text comprehension** | Vocabulary, inference, situation model, monitoring | Expert reading for meaning |

**Central thesis:** phonics is **necessary and non-negotiable** in alphabetic systems but **insufficient** — fluent word recognition + comprehension required. Reading wars persist partly because (a) *why* phonics works is under-explained publicly, and (b) phonics is framed as the whole program rather than foundation.

## Operationalization (RU written v1 / von Neumann program)

- **Bootstrap:** systematic **print** path (GPC + PA with letters) → orthographic mapping endpoint (Ehri 2014 card); not phonological-only programs (Stalega 2024 card)
- **Gate D:** held-out word + nonword decode on field; fade analytic scaffold per mapping success
- **Gate C:** held-out **word in sentence** / short passage — never phonics drill pass alone
- **Program arc:** Castles §1→§2→§3 mirrors curriculum waves: code → fluency → comprehension strategies + volume of reading
- **RU depth:** expect **slower early GPC consolidation** than shallow orthographies; do not skip print path; timing from RU evidence cards, not English policy copy
- **Anti-pattern:** infinite GPC without fluency fade; decodable-only past early phase; comprehension-only intervention when D weak (SVR)

## Limitations

- Tutorial review, not new RCTs; English-centric examples predominate
- Policy sections (England phonics check) = illustration, not RU mandate
- Decodable-text tipping point explicitly **under-researched** per authors
