## Fundamentals

NRP (14-member panel, 2000) synthesized experimental literature into **five instructional components** for integrated reading programs:

1. Phonemic awareness (PA)  
2. Phonics  
3. Fluency  
4. Vocabulary  
5. Text comprehension strategies  

**No single component = complete program.** Phonics/PA are foundation, not ceiling.

## Extracted knowledge (K)

### Phonemic awareness

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K1 | Explicit PA instruction improves reading; effective across ages/abilities |
| K2 | **Best PA:** manipulate phonemes **with letters**; focus 1–2 manipulation types; small groups |
| K3 | PA necessary foundation but **not** complete reading program |
| K4 | PA improves spelling in normals; **not** effective for disabled readers' spelling |
| K5 | Effects last beyond training; no math-test halo (causal specificity) |
| K6 | 52 studies → 96 comparisons in PA meta-analysis |

### Phonics

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K7 | Systematic phonics >> little/no phonics (38 studies, 66 comparisons) |
| K8 | Benefits K–6; helps disabled readers; **synthetic phonics** notable for at-risk/low SES |
| K9 | K–1: improves decode, spell, **and** comprehension; 2–6: oral reading gains, **comprehension not significantly improved** by phonics alone |
| K10 | Kindergarten phonics effective — conventional “not ready” unsupported |
| K11 | Phonics types defined: synthetic, analytic, embedded, analogy, spelling-through, onset-rime |
| K12 | Must apply GPC knowledge in reading/writing — programs heavy on rules without application ineffective |
| K13 | Caution on undefined “intensive” phonics — duration/scope open research questions |
| K14 | Teacher knowledge + flexible pacing within systematic frame critical |

### Fluency

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K15 | **Guided repeated oral reading** with feedback → significant gains in word recognition, fluency, comprehension (16-study meta) |
| K16 | **Independent silent reading programs alone** — **insufficient evidence** for achievement/fluency gains (14 studies, no meta; no causal proof) |
| K17 | ISR may help when combined; not sole fluency instruction for children without alphabetic skills |

### Comprehension — vocabulary

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K18 | Vocabulary instruction → comprehension gains; direct + indirect teaching |
| K19 | Repetition, rich contexts, computer aids, task restructuring help |
| K20 | Single-method vocabulary instruction suboptimal |

### Comprehension — text strategies

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K21 | **Seven strategy types** with solid basis: monitoring, cooperative learning, graphic organizers, Q&A, question generation, story structure, summarization |
| K22 | **Combination** of strategies most effective |
| K23 | Content-area comprehension instruction may be efficient (open question) |

### Teacher preparation

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K24 | Inservice PD with student outcomes shows significant achievement gains |
| K25 | Preservice data thin; comprehension strategy teaching needs extensive formal training |

### Technology

| # | Тезис |
|---|--------|
| K26 | Computer-assisted reading instruction promising (21 studies, all positive); speech + hypertext noted; Internet gap |

## Limitations

- Findings layer ≠ full subgroup reports with every effect size
- US English 1990s corpus
- ISR null finding often misread as “reading volume useless” — NRP nuance: **programs encouraging ISR** not proven causal
