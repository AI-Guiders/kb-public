## Fundamentals

**Question:** Is PA instruction **without print** as effective for reading outcomes as **print-based** instruction (phonics)?

**Sample:** 38 independent studies, 3,880 participants, mean age 5y7m, preschool–grade 1.

---

## Extracted knowledge (K)

| # | Тезис | Stat |
|---|--------|------|
| K1 | PA-only has **moderate** effect on reading-related outcomes overall | g = 0.57, 95% CI [0.40, 0.74] |
| K2 | PA-only **less effective than print-based** instruction | g = **−0.54**, 95% CI [−0.80, −0.27] vs phonics/print |
| K3 | PA-only improves **PA outcomes** more than **word reading** | PA g = 0.64 vs word reading g = −0.33 (marginal) |
| K4 | Stronger effects in **K and grade 1** than preschool | moderator summary (Best Evidence in Brief) |
| K5 | At-risk vs typical — no special case for PA-only necessity | conclusion per secondary summaries |
| K6 | Aligns with NRP: best PA manipulates phonemes **with letters** (Ehri PA meta; NRP card) | cross-card |

## Operationalization (RU written v1)

- **Bootstrap:** PA/grapheme work **with print on field** — oral segmentation alone is supporting skill, not program core
- Elkonin boxes / analytic phases → must bond to **letters/words**, fade to orthographic mapping
- Gate: word reading held-out, not PA task pass alone
- **Deny:** curriculum that stops at phonological training without systematic print path
