## Design (what was measured)

- **N = 259** UK English-speaking children, **longitudinal** Year 3 → Year 4 (≈ US 2nd → 3rd grade).
- **Basic calculation proficiency:** timed simple **addition/subtraction** (0–9 operands); accuracy + strategy coding (retrieve vs count/decompose).
- **Math achievement:** WIAT-II (UK) **Numerical Operations** + **Mathematical Reasoning** (combined z-score).
- **Conceptual knowledge:** number line 0–1000; **nonverbal calculation** (objects, no counting aloud).
- **Cognitive:** WM (CE span, visuospatial WM, speed); **oral language** — TROG (grammar), BPVS (vocabulary); reading (WIAT word reading).
- **Controls / covariates:** age, gender, SES proxy (school meals), special educational needs flags.

## Main findings (empirical, not prescriptive for CASA)

1. **High correlation** between basic calculation and math achievement in both years (authors report r ≈ .70 range; regression β substantial) — relation is **well established** in sample.
2. **Most children did not retrieve** answers to most problems in either grade — yet **math achievement was typical** for age (national norms). Challenges **traditional** view that fluent retrieval is prerequisite for normal achievement at this stage.
3. **Improvement** in basic calculation over one year was **partially predicted** by conceptual knowledge and cognitive factors (WM, speed, oral language components in models).
4. **Mediation (Baron & Kenny + Sobel):** conceptual knowledge + cognitive factors **partially** mediate calculation ↔ achievement; **not full** mediation — direct/shared path remains.
5. **Reading ↔ math:** relation **wholly mediated** through number measures + cognitive factors (not direct reading→math path in final model).
6. **Oral language:** TROG (grammar) and BPVS contribute in regression paths to calculation and/or achievement; authors frame **language as plausible contributor** alongside WM and number knowledge — not sole driver.

## Interpretive frames (authors)

Three educational perspectives discussed:

- **Traditional:** retrieval fluency → math achievement (partially supported by correlation, **weakened** by typical achievement despite low retrieval).
- **Reform / number sense:** conceptual knowledge and flexible strategies matter (supported as **partial** predictors and mediators).
- **Cognitive:** WM, speed, oral language as resources for calculation and broader math (supported; **partial** mediation).

## Limits (honest)

- UK sample, one-year lag; observational longitudinal — **not** intervention on retrieval training.
- WIAT composite masks subscale differences.
- Strategy coding from timed tasks — retrieval under time pressure ≠ untimed competence.
- Co-author Saxton ≠ this paper tests **negative evidence / recasts** (see language-acquisition cards).
